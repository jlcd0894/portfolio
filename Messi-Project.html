The Messi Store™

A multi-disciplinary approach to a legendary celebration

The Messi Store is the online store for The Messi Brand, the official premium lifestyle brand of the legendary soccer player Leo Messi. Their primary focus is to integrate the values, vision, and sportsmanship that Leo is known for on the pitch into their apparel, as well as keeping these aspects at the core of their brand.

Client

The Messi Store

Completed

2021

Role

Ui/Ux Design, Graphic Design

The Mission

To envision, design, and develop a campaign celebrating Leo's most recent success, winnning "La Copa America", to push forward and expand The Messi Brand and The Messi Store. The main focus was on designing a series of postal cards with emblematic designs of Leo's career, to be given away for free. A simple landing page needed to be designed and developed so the customers could safely get their postal cards. The main purpose of this project was to expand the brand, create new customers, and test the marketability of The Messi Brand.

Sketches

The first step was to create a landing page that could display and sell the postal cards. There was no need to create a site map for this project as only one page was needed. The idea was to make the entire process seamless, that's why we decided to make a page that contained both the product display, and the checkout. Having a hybrid page like this makes it easier for the customer, and avoids any potential traffic leaks.

Low Fidelity

The low fidelity wireframe for this project was very straight forward, a single landing page that included both the product and the checkout.

Style Guide

This style guide was created using the brand guidelines of "The Messi Brand", putting into consideration that this is a relatively new brand, it is important to keep in line with their aesthetics so the customers learn to identify the brand just by looking at the elements on the landing page.

Finishing the design

Using "The Messi Brand" guidelines and aesthetics, I created the high fidelity wireframe, this prototype was sent to, and reviewed by stakeholders to make sure everything was in order before the development process started.

Rejected Designs

The postal card design process was very complex, a lot of do's and dont's, several guidelines to follow, and multiple rounds of revisions to ensure everything was ready for print. Below you can see a series of rejected designs, I wanted to display these to show the amount of work that I put into them. It's worth mentioning that the reason these were rejected was because of legal issues, showing extertnal brands, logos and similarities. We were told that even though they liked the designs, it was risky to use them as they could present legal problems.

End Product

After multiple rounds of designs and reviews, the stakeholders chose 3 designs as the final product which you can see below. These were chosen from the original "rejected" designs, and were modified to a point where no other logos were present in order to avoid any potential legasl issues. A set of media posts was also cerated, displaying the postal cards so they could be posted on launch day to Facebook and Instagram, 1 set for The Messi Store page, and another for Leo Messi's personal accounts.
